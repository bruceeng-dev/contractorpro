ARTICLE 5: CHANGE ORDERS & MODIFICATIONS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

5.1 CHANGE ORDER REQUIREMENT

NO modifications to the Work, Contract Price, or completion date shall
be valid unless documented in a written Change Order signed by both
parties. Verbal agreements, text messages, or email discussions are NOT
binding without a formal Change Order.

5.2 CHANGE ORDER PRICING

Additional Work shall be priced as follows:

Labor Rates (includes overhead & profit):
• Licensed Electrician:        $125/hour (2-hour minimum)
• Licensed Plumber:             $135/hour (2-hour minimum)
• Skilled Carpenter:            $85/hour (2-hour minimum)
• General Laborer:              $45/hour
• Project Management:           15% of subcontractor costs

Materials & Equipment:
• Materials:                    Actual cost + 15% markup
• Equipment rental:             Actual cost + 10% markup
• Delivery charges:             Pass-through at cost

5.3 CHANGE ORDER PROCESS

1. Owner or Contractor identifies proposed change
2. Contractor prepares written Change Order with:
   - Detailed description of modified work
   - Cost impact (increase or decrease)
   - Schedule impact (days added/subtracted)
3. Owner reviews and approves, rejects, or negotiates
4. Both parties sign Change Order
5. Work proceeds only after signed approval
6. Payment for change work included in next progress invoice

5.4 CONCEALED CONDITIONS

If concealed conditions are discovered that differ materially from those
indicated in the Contract Documents or reasonably anticipated, Contractor
shall:

a) Stop Work in affected area immediately
b) Notify Owner within 24 hours of discovery
c) Propose Change Order for remediation if required
d) Resume Work only after Owner approval

Examples: Hidden rot, mold, asbestos, substandard wiring, plumbing leaks,
structural deficiencies, incorrect as-built conditions.

ARTICLE 7: WARRANTY & DEFECTS LIABILITY
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

7.1 WORKMANSHIP WARRANTY

Contractor warrants all labor and workmanship for a period of ONE (1)
YEAR from the date of Substantial Completion. This warranty covers:

• Defects in installation and workmanship
• Proper performance of all systems (electrical, plumbing, HVAC)
• Structural integrity of all framing and modifications
• Proper function of all installed fixtures and equipment

7.2 WARRANTY EXCLUSIONS

This warranty does NOT cover:

a) Normal wear and tear, settlement, or shrinkage of materials
b) Damage caused by Owner abuse, misuse, or negligence
c) Damage from failure to maintain (clogged gutters, deferred maintenance)
d) Modifications or repairs performed by others
e) Acts of God, fire, flood, earthquake, or force majeure events
f) Owner-supplied materials or equipment
g) Manufacturer defects (covered by manufacturer warranty)
h) Cosmetic issues that do not affect function

7.3 MANUFACTURER WARRANTIES

All materials and equipment are covered by manufacturer warranties which
pass through to Owner. Contractor will:
• Provide all warranty documentation at project closeout
• Assist with warranty claims but is not liable for manufacturer defects
• Replace defective materials during construction at no cost

7.4 WARRANTY SERVICE PROCEDURE

To make a warranty claim, Owner shall:
1. Notify Contractor in writing (email acceptable)
2. Describe the defect with photos if possible
3. Provide reasonable access for inspection

Contractor shall:
1. Respond within FIVE (5) business days
2. Schedule repair at mutually convenient time
3. Complete repair within TEN (10) business days
4. Emergency repairs (leaks, electrical hazards) within 24 hours

7.5 WARRANTY LIMITATIONS

Contractor's maximum liability under this warranty is limited to the
actual cost of repairing or replacing the defective work. Contractor
is not liable for:
• Consequential or indirect damages
• Lost profits or business interruption
• Temporary housing or relocation costs
• Diminution in property value

ARTICLE 10: SITE CONDITIONS & PROTECTION
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

10.1 SITE PREPARATION

Contractor shall:
• Establish protected work zones with barriers
• Install floor and wall protection in access paths
• Provide secure storage for materials and tools
• Maintain safe egress routes per OSHA standards

10.2 ENVIRONMENTAL CONTROLS

Contractor shall provide reasonable dust control measures including:
• Plastic barriers with zipper access where feasible
• HEPA filtration in active work areas
• Sealing of HVAC registers to prevent dust migration
• Daily cleanup of work debris

IMPORTANT: Despite best efforts, construction generates dust and debris.
Some dust migration to non-work areas is unavoidable. Owner should
relocate sensitive items and plan for post-construction deep cleaning.

10.3 UTILITY MANAGEMENT

Contractor shall:
• Identify and mark existing utilities before demolition
• Coordinate utility shut-offs with 24-hour advance notice to Owner
• Protect active utilities from damage
• Cap and secure exposed utilities during construction

10.4 PROPERTY PROTECTION

Contractor shall exercise reasonable care to protect:
• Existing structures and finishes to remain
• Owner's personal property in work areas
• Landscaping along access routes
• Adjacent properties from construction activities

Contractor is NOT responsible for:
• Pre-existing damage or defects
• Owner's failure to relocate valuables or personal property
• Minor cosmetic damage (nail pops, hairline cracks) outside work area
• Damage to underground utilities not marked by utility locator

10.5 HAZARDOUS MATERIALS

If suspect hazardous materials are discovered (asbestos, lead, mold):
a) Work shall STOP immediately in affected area
b) Owner shall be notified within 24 hours
c) Owner shall contract for testing and abatement if required
d) Contractor resumes after clearance by certified abatement contractor
e) Delay costs due to hazmat discovery are Owner's responsibility

ARTICLE 11: DISPUTE RESOLUTION & GOVERNING LAW
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

11.1 GOOD FAITH NEGOTIATION

Prior to formal dispute resolution, parties agree to meet in good faith
to attempt resolution through direct negotiation. Either party may
request a meeting within FIVE (5) business days of written request.

11.2 MEDIATION

If negotiation fails, parties agree to participate in non-binding
mediation before pursuing arbitration or litigation. Mediator shall be
selected by mutual agreement or through local dispute resolution center.
Mediation costs split equally between parties.

11.3 BINDING ARBITRATION

Disputes exceeding FIVE THOUSAND DOLLARS ($5,000) that cannot be
resolved through mediation shall be resolved through binding arbitration
conducted under the Construction Industry Arbitration Rules of the
American Arbitration Association (AAA).

Arbitration Terms:
• Venue: [COUNTY], [STATE]
• One arbitrator selected per AAA procedures
• Discovery permitted per arbitrator's discretion
• Arbitrator's decision is final and binding
• Judgment may be entered in any court of competent jurisdiction

11.4 SMALL CLAIMS JURISDICTION

Disputes under FIVE THOUSAND DOLLARS ($5,000) may be filed in small
claims court without requirement for mediation or arbitration.

11.5 ATTORNEY FEES & COSTS

The prevailing party in any dispute resolution proceeding (including
negotiation, mediation, arbitration, or litigation) shall be entitled
to recover reasonable attorney fees, expert witness fees, court costs,
and other related expenses from the non-prevailing party.

11.6 GOVERNING LAW

This Contract shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the
laws of the State of [STATE], without regard to conflicts of law
provisions. Venue for any legal proceedings shall be [COUNTY], [STATE].

11.7 WAIVER OF JURY TRIAL

Both parties hereby knowingly and voluntarily WAIVE their right to trial
by jury for any dispute arising under this Contract.

ARTICLE 12: TERMINATION
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

12.1 TERMINATION FOR CAUSE

Either party may terminate this Contract for material breach after
providing TEN (10) days written notice specifying the breach and
opportunity to cure. Material breaches include but are not limited to:

By Owner:
• Failure to make payment within thirty (30) days of due date
• Repeated denial of access to work areas
• Interference with Contractor's ability to perform

By Contractor:
• Abandonment of Work for more than ten (10) consecutive days
• Repeated failure to comply with building codes
• Failure to correct deficiencies after written notice

12.2 TERMINATION FOR CONVENIENCE (OWNER)

Owner may terminate this Contract at any time for any reason upon
SEVEN (7) days written notice to Contractor. Upon termination:

Owner shall pay:
a) All completed Work at Contract rate
b) Materials purchased or on order (with invoices)
c) Equipment rental and cancellation fees
d) TEN PERCENT (10%) of remaining contract value as liquidated damages

12.3 TERMINATION FOR CONVENIENCE (CONTRACTOR)

Contractor may terminate if:
a) Owner fails to make payment after thirty (30) days notice
b) Owner repeatedly interferes with Work progress
c) Work is stopped by legal authority through no fault of Contractor

Upon termination, Owner pays for all Work completed and materials
purchased. Contractor shall remove tools and secure site.

12.4 EFFECT OF TERMINATION

Upon termination, Contractor shall:
• Stop Work immediately (except for site securing)
• Remove tools, equipment, and unused materials
• Provide documentation of work completed and costs incurred
• Provide conditional lien waiver upon receipt of final payment

Owner shall:
• Make final payment within thirty (30) days
• Assume responsibility for completing remaining Work
• Indemnify Contractor for claims arising after termination
