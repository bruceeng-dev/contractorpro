## 3. DETAILED SCOPE OF WORK (BY DISCIPLINE)

The following work shall be performed by Contractor or Contractor's authorized subcontractors:

### 3.1 Site Preparation & Protection

* **Access and Staging:**
  - Establish protected pathways and work zones
  - Install temporary barriers, floor protection, and wall protection
  - Provide secure storage for materials and tools on-site
  - Maintain safe egress routes per OSHA requirements

* **Environmental Controls:**
  - Install temporary dust partitions with zipper access where feasible
  - Deploy negative air machines with HEPA filtration in active work zones
  - Seal HVAC registers in work areas to prevent dust migration
  - Provide adequate ventilation during finish work

* **Utility Protection:**
  - Identify and mark all existing utilities prior to demolition
  - Coordinate utility shut-offs with Owner (minimum 24-hour notice)
  - Protect active utilities from damage during construction
  - Provide temporary power, lighting, and ventilation as needed

### 3.2 Demolition & Removal

[DEMOLITION_WORK]

* **Selective Demolition:**
  - Remove only materials specifically identified in scope
  - Protect adjacent areas and finishes to remain
  - Cut and patch per industry standards to minimize collateral damage
  - Cap, label, and secure all utilities during demolition phase

* **Hazardous Materials:**
  - Visual inspection for potential asbestos-containing materials (ACM) or lead-based paint
  - If suspect materials identified, work will STOP pending Owner-contracted testing
  - Contractor not responsible for hazmat abatement (see Exclusions)

* **Disposal & Recycling:**
  - Load-out debris daily or as space permits
  - Haul all demolition waste to licensed disposal facility
  - Provide dump receipts upon Owner request
  - Recycle materials where economically feasible (metal, cardboard, clean wood)
  - Separate and properly dispose of hazardous waste (paint, solvents, batteries)

### 3.3 Structural & Framing Work

[STRUCTURAL_WORK]

* **Structural Modifications:**
  - Install engineered beams, headers, and support columns per stamped plans (if applicable)
  - Obtain structural engineer review for load-bearing alterations
  - Temporary shoring and bracing during structural work
  - Anchor and fasten per IRC/IBC specifications

* **Rough Framing:**
  - Frame new walls, soffits, and blocking per approved drawings
  - Use dimensional lumber meeting or exceeding Grade 2 or better
  - Install metal framing where specified for wet areas or fire-rated assemblies
  - Frame openings to accommodate specified door and window sizes
  - Provide backing and blocking for cabinets, fixtures, and grab bars

* **Inspections:**
  - Schedule and obtain framing inspection approval
  - Correct any inspector-noted deficiencies
  - Provide Engineer's Letter of Compliance if structural work performed

### 3.4 Electrical Work (MEP)

[ELECTRICAL_WORK]

* **Rough Electrical:**
  - Run circuits per National Electrical Code (NEC) current edition
  - Install outlet boxes, junction boxes, and device boxes at code-compliant heights
  - Provide AFCI protection for bedrooms, GFCI protection for wet locations
  - Upgrade panel capacity if existing service inadequate (priced separately if discovered)
  - Label all circuits at panel and in attic/crawl spaces

* **Trim Electrical:**
  - Install all switches, outlets, and cover plates
  - Install light fixtures per allowance or Owner-supplied
  - Install ceiling fans with proper bracing and control switches
  - Test all circuits for proper polarity, grounding, and GFCI function
  - Provide panel schedule and circuit directory

* **Permits & Inspections:**
  - Obtain electrical permit and coordinate rough/final inspections
  - Provide temporary power during construction
  - Correct all inspector-noted deficiencies at no cost to Owner

* **Code Compliance:**
  - AFCI breakers for all 15A and 20A bedroom circuits
  - GFCI protection for kitchen countertop, bathroom, garage, exterior outlets
  - Tamper-resistant receptacles in areas accessible to children
  - Arc-fault and ground-fault testing with documentation

### 3.5 Plumbing Work (MEP)

[PLUMBING_WORK]

* **Rough Plumbing:**
  - Relocate or extend water supply lines using PEX, copper, or CPVC per code
  - Install drain, waste, and vent (DWV) piping per UPC/IPC
  - Provide proper venting for all fixtures and drain slope per code
  - Install hose bibs, shut-off valves, and access panels where required
  - Pressure test all supply lines; drain test all waste lines

* **Finish Plumbing:**
  - Set toilets, sinks, faucets, and shower fixtures per allowance or Owner-supplied
  - Install angle stops, supply lines, and P-traps
  - Connect and test garbage disposals, dishwashers, and other appliances
  - Caulk and seal all penetrations and fixture bases
  - Final water flow and leak inspection

* **Permits & Inspections:**
  - Obtain plumbing permit and coordinate inspections
  - Provide backflow prevention if required by jurisdiction
  - Correct all inspector-noted deficiencies

### 3.6 HVAC (Heating, Ventilation, Air Conditioning)

[HVAC_WORK]

* **Ductwork Modifications:**
  - Reroute supply and return ducts to accommodate new layout
  - Seal all duct joints with mastic (not duct tape)
  - Insulate ducts in unconditioned spaces to R-6 or better
  - Restore vapor barriers at all penetrations

* **Ventilation:**
  - Install bath exhaust fans ducted to exterior (no attic termination)
  - Provide range hood vented to exterior or recirculating per Owner preference
  - Install fresh air intake if required by code for tight construction

* **Balance & Commissioning:**
  - Balance airflow after completion to ensure even temperature distribution
  - Test all registers for proper CFM delivery
  - Provide Owner with filter replacement schedule

### 3.7 Insulation & Drywall

* **Insulation:**
  - Install or replace insulation at all opened walls and ceilings
  - Meet or exceed current energy code R-values for climate zone
  - Use fiberglass batts, blown-in cellulose, or spray foam per specification
  - Vapor barrier installation per local code requirements
  - Seal all penetrations and gaps to prevent thermal bridging

* **Drywall Installation:**
  - Hang 1/2" or 5/8" drywall per code (5/8" Type X for fire-rated assemblies)
  - Tape, mud, and sand to Level 4 finish (smooth, ready for paint)
  - Use moisture-resistant (MR) or mold-resistant drywall in wet areas
  - Provide smooth, defect-free surfaces; repair any imperfections
  - Install corner beads on all outside corners

* **Texturing:**
  - Match existing texture or provide smooth finish as specified
  - Orange peel, knockdown, or skip trowel textures available
  - Ceiling texture to match existing adjacent areas

### 3.8 Cabinets, Trim & Millwork

[MILLWORK_DETAILS]

* **Cabinet Installation:**
  - Install cabinets level, plumb, and securely fastened to wall studs
  - Shim and scribe cabinets for perfect fit
  - Install soft-close hinges and drawer glides
  - Adjust doors and drawers for smooth operation
  - Touch up any installation damage with factory-matched finish

* **Trim & Molding:**
  - Install baseboard, casing, crown molding per design
  - Miter corners precisely; caulk nail holes and seams
  - Use paint-grade MDF or stain-grade hardwood per specification
  - Match existing profiles in adjacent spaces or upgrade as specified
  - Install door and window trim with reveals consistent throughout

* **Specialty Millwork:**
  - Built-in shelving, benches, or custom features per drawings
  - Install with proper anchoring and structural support
  - Pre-finish or prepare for paint per Owner selection

### 3.9 Countertops & Tile Work

[COUNTERTOP_TILE_WORK]

* **Countertop Installation:**
  - Template after cabinet installation for precise fit
  - Fabricate and install per allowance material selection
  - Provide polished edges, backsplash, and cutouts for sink/cooktop
  - Undermount or drop-in sink installation per design
  - Seal natural stone per manufacturer recommendations

* **Tile Installation:**
  - Prepare substrate with cement board or Kerdi waterproof membrane in wet areas
  - Install tile with proper thinset adhesive and grout per TCNA standards
  - Provide expansion joints and proper slope in shower floors
  - Seal grout in wet areas with penetrating sealer
  - Caulk inside corners and transitions (never grout)

* **Backsplash & Accent Tile:**
  - Install kitchen backsplash to full height or 4" above countertop per design
  - Align tile layout for symmetrical appearance
  - Cut tile cleanly around outlets and fixtures
  - Use bullnose or Schluter trim for finished edges

### 3.10 Flooring & Painting

[FLOORING_PAINT_WORK]

* **Flooring Preparation:**
  - Remove existing flooring and inspect subfloor for damage
  - Repair or replace damaged subfloor sheathing
  - Level subfloor within 3/16" over 10 feet
  - Install appropriate underlayment (cement board, moisture barrier, foam)

* **Flooring Installation:**
  - Install hardwood, LVP, tile, or carpet per specification
  - Provide proper expansion gaps for floating floors
  - Nail hardwood per manufacturer nailing schedule
  - Install transitions, reducers, and thresholds at doorways
  - Final sand and finish hardwood with 3 coats polyurethane (if applicable)

* **Painting:**
  - Prime all new drywall, wood, and previously unpainted surfaces
  - Apply two finish coats of premium interior paint
  - Cut in cleanly at ceilings, corners, and trim
  - Use paint sprayer or roller for smooth, even finish
  - Touch up after final fixture installation

* **Paint Schedule:**
  - Walls: [WALL_PAINT_SPEC] (e.g., Sherwin Williams Duration, Eggshell)
  - Trim: [TRIM_PAINT_SPEC] (e.g., Semi-gloss white enamel)
  - Ceilings: [CEILING_PAINT_SPEC] (e.g., Flat white ceiling paint)

### 3.11 Exterior Work (if applicable)

[EXTERIOR_WORK]

* **Exterior Repairs:**
  - Repair or replace damaged siding, trim, or roofing
  - Flash all penetrations and roof/wall transitions
  - Caulk and seal all exterior openings
  - Paint or stain exterior surfaces to match existing

* **Roof Work:**
  - Install roof jacks for plumbing vents with proper flashing
  - Repair or replace damaged shingles to match existing
  - Ensure proper attic ventilation (ridge vent, soffit vents)

* **Exterior Finishes:**
  - Install exterior doors and windows per energy code
  - Install exterior light fixtures and outlets
  - Slope grade away from foundation for proper drainage

### 3.12 Permits, Inspections & Closeout

* **Permit Coordination:**
  - Research and obtain all required building permits
  - Pay permit fees (included in contract price unless otherwise noted)
  - Post permits visibly at job site
  - Schedule all required inspections (framing, MEP rough, insulation, final)

* **Inspection Management:**
  - Coordinate inspector access and walkthrough
  - Correct all inspector punch items promptly
  - Obtain signed-off permit cards and Certificates of Occupancy
  - Provide copies of all inspection reports to Owner

* **Closeout Documentation:**
  - **Final lien waivers** from Contractor and all subcontractors
  - **Manufacturer warranties** for appliances, fixtures, and equipment
  - **Care & maintenance instructions** for finishes and materials
  - **As-built notes** showing concealed utility routing, shutoffs, and access panels
  - **Paint & finish schedule** with color names and product specifications
  - **Photo log** of concealed MEP work (in-wall plumbing, electrical routing)
  - **Owner's manual** with emergency shutoffs, circuit panel directory, and HVAC filter info

* **Final Walkthrough:**
  - Schedule walkthrough with Owner upon substantial completion
  - Create punch list of remaining items requiring correction
  - Complete all punch items within 10 business days
  - Obtain Owner signature on Completion Certificate

## 9. QUALITY STANDARDS & ACCEPTANCE CRITERIA

All work shall meet or exceed the following standards:

**General Workmanship:**
* Work performed in a professional manner consistent with industry best practices
* All surfaces smooth, plumb, level, square, and free of defects
* Materials installed per manufacturer specifications and recommendations
* All work complies with applicable building codes and permit requirements

**Electrical Standards:**
* All circuits labeled at panel and tested for proper function
* AFCI/GFCI devices tested and functioning correctly
* Outlets installed at consistent heights (12" AFF standard, 42" countertop)
* Switches installed at 48" AFF unless otherwise specified
* Light fixtures installed level and securely fastened
* Panel schedule updated and legible

**Plumbing Standards:**
* All supply lines pressure tested to 80 PSI for 2 hours minimum
* All drain lines tested for leaks and proper flow
* Hot water on left, cold water on right (facing fixture)
* All traps properly vented per code
* No leaks at any connection points
* Proper fixture operation and water flow

**Finish Standards:**
* Drywall smooth and defect-free (Level 4 finish minimum)
* Paint coverage even with no holidays, runs, or brush marks
* Tile grout lines consistent in width (1/16" to 1/8" typical)
* Trim joints tight with minimal caulking required
* Cabinet doors and drawers adjusted for smooth operation
* All surfaces clean and free of construction debris

**Acceptance Process:**
* Contractor notifies Owner of Substantial Completion
* Owner walkthrough scheduled within 5 business days
* Punch list created documenting remaining items
* Contractor completes punch list within 10 business days
* Final walkthrough and Owner acceptance signature
* Final payment due per contract terms

**Concealed Conditions:**
